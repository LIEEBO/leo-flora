There’s a moment at every wedding when the bride has to set her bouquet down. On a chair, a windowsill, the corner of a sweetheart table where it sits forgotten through dinner. The purse bouquet solves that, and it does something the bouquet never could: it goes with her. Down the aisle, through the first dance, and right into the reception. It’s a hands-free, stylish, and practical alternative that’s winning over DIY brides and wedding planners alike.

## What Is a Purse Bouquet?

A purse bouquet is exactly what it sounds like: a small, handheld purse or clutch that’s adorned with flowers, greenery, and sometimes even ribbon or jewels. Instead of carrying a traditional bouquet, you carry a functional accessory that doubles as floral art. It clips onto your wrist or sits in your hand, leaving you free to hug guests, adjust your dress, or hold a glass of champagne. For brides who want to keep their hands free without sacrificing style, this is a game-changer.

## Why Purse Bouquets Are Perfect for DIY Brides

If you’re planning your own wedding, you know every detail matters. Purse bouquets are surprisingly easy to make at home. You can start with a simple clutch or wristlet from a thrift store or craft shop, then attach artificial flowers for wedding decor. Because you’re using faux blooms, the bouquet stays perfect all day—no wilting, no water, no stress. Plus, you can match the flowers to your wedding flower wall backdrop or centerpieces for a cohesive look. Many DIY brides love using wholesale silk flowers to create multiple purse bouquets for the bridal party, saving money and time.

## How to Style a Purse Bouquet for Your Wedding

Purse bouquets work for any wedding style. For a boho beach wedding, choose a woven clutch with dried flowers and eucalyptus. For a classic ballroom affair, opt for a satin or pearl-encrusted purse with roses and hydrangeas. The key is to keep the arrangement compact so it doesn’t overwhelm the purse. Use floral foam or a small water tube inside the clutch to secure stems. If you’re using artificial flowers for wedding decor, you can skip the water and glue stems directly into place. This also means you can prep your bouquet weeks in advance.

## Practical Tips for Making Your Own Purse Bouquet

Here are some tips to get started:
- **Choose the right purse**: Look for a clutch with a flat top or a wristlet with a sturdy handle. Avoid oversized bags that will look bulky.
- **Pick your flowers**: Use a mix of focal flowers (like roses or peonies) and filler (like baby’s breath or lavender). Wholesale silk flowers are perfect because they come in bulk and are easy to trim.
- **Attach securely**: Use a hot glue gun to attach flowers to the purse. For a more secure hold, wrap stems with floral tape first.
- **Add greenery**: Ferns, ivy, or eucalyptus add texture and soften the look. Tuck them around the edges of the purse.
- **Coordinate with your decor**: If you have a wedding flower wall backdrop, use the same flower types and colors in your purse bouquet for a unified aesthetic.
- **Practice carrying it**: Before the big day, walk around with the purse to make sure it feels comfortable and doesn’t snag on your dress.
