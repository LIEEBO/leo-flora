When we think of a classic New England summer wedding, images of historic estates, ocean breezes, and gardens in full bloom come to mind. The recent Castle Hill Inn wedding captured that spirit perfectly—a romantic, airy coastal garden-party by the sea. If you're planning a similar event and want to preserve that dreamy aesthetic without the stress of wilting blooms, you're in the right place. In this article, we'll break down the design elements from this real wedding and show you how to recreate them using faux floral arrangements, a stunning wedding flower wall backdrop, and elegant fake flowers centerpiece ideas that will wow your guests.

## Capturing the Coastal Garden Vibe with Faux Florals

The original wedding featured a palette of white, ivory, green, and pale blue—colors that evoke the serenity of the Narragansett Bay. To achieve this look with artificial flowers, focus on high-quality silk blooms that mimic the softness of real petals. Look for white roses, ivory hydrangeas, and pale blue delphiniums or hydrangeas. Mix in eucalyptus and ivy for that lush garden feel. Faux floral arrangements allow you to create the same airy, romantic look without worrying about weather or timing. For the ceremony space, consider a wedding flower wall backdrop in soft greens and whites to frame your vows against the ocean view.

## Designing a Fake Flowers Centerpiece That Steals the Show

Centerpieces are the heart of your reception tables. For a coastal garden theme, think low, lush arrangements in ceramic or glass vessels. A fake flowers centerpiece can include a mix of ivory peonies, white garden roses, and pale blue thistle, all nestled in trailing greenery. Add touches of baby's breath for that ethereal, cloud-like effect. The beauty of using faux stems is that you can pre-assemble them weeks in advance, ensuring every table looks perfect on the day. To keep it budget-friendly, use a combination of premium silk flowers and realistic greenery from craft stores.

## Creating a Wedding Flower Wall Backdrop for Photos and Ceremony

A wedding flower wall backdrop is a must-have for photo opportunities and ceremony backdrops. For the Castle Hill Inn vibe, you can build a panel of faux greenery—like boxwood, ivy, and ferns—and then insert clusters of white and pale blue flowers. This creates a lush, garden-like setting that photographs beautifully and lasts all day. You can make it modular so it's easy to transport and set up. Add string lights or sheer fabric for extra romance. This backdrop will be a focal point for your guests and a perfect spot for group photos.

## Practical Tips for DIY Brides and Planners

- **Start early**: Begin sourcing your faux floral arrangements at least 2-3 months before the wedding. This gives you time to order samples and match colors.
- **Mix textures**: Combine matte and glossy faux flowers for realism. Add velvet or silk leaves for depth.
- **Use floral foam**: For centerpieces, use wet foam blocks to keep stems secure, even if you're using fake flowers. This helps with stability.
- **Scale matters**: For a large estate like Castle Hill Inn, go big with your arrangements. Oversized bouquets and tall centerpieces fill the space elegantly.
- **Photography tip**: Faux flowers often look even better in photos than real ones because they hold their shape and color. Test your arrangements under different lighting.
- **Budget wisely**: Invest in statement pieces like the wedding flower wall backdrop and a few hero centerpieces. For less visible spots, use more affordable greenery.

## Final Thoughts

A coastal garden wedding doesn't have to be limited by season or budget. By choosing faux floral arrangements, a stunning fake flowers centerpiece, and a custom wedding flower wall backdrop, you can capture the same romantic, airy spirit of a Newport summer—without the wilt. Whether you're a DIY bride or a seasoned planner, these tips will help you create a timeless, beautiful celebration that feels fresh and effortless. Remember, the best weddings are those that reflect your style and make you feel joy—and with artificial flowers, that joy lasts long after the last dance.
