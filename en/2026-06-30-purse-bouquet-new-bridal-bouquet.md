## What Is a Purse Bouquet?
A purse bouquet is exactly what it sounds like: a small, structured handbag or clutch that doubles as a bouquet. Instead of holding a traditional round or cascading arrangement, the bride carries a purse adorned with florals. The flowers are arranged directly on the bag, often with a handle or wrist strap, so the bride can wear it like an accessory. It’s a clever twist on the classic bridal bouquet that solves the age-old problem of what to do with your flowers when you need both hands. Whether you’re walking down the aisle or greeting guests, the purse bouquet stays with you. ## How to Create Your Own Purse Bouquet
Making a purse bouquet is easier than you think, and it’s a fun DIY project for brides or planners. Start with a sturdy clutch or small handbag—thrift stores are great for finding unique options. Then, gather your materials: **wholesale silk flowers** (roses, peonies, or baby’s breath work beautifully), floral wire, hot glue, and a pair of clippers. Begin by trimming the stems to about 2 inches and wiring them for stability. Arrange the flowers on the front of the purse in a cluster or cascade, securing each piece with hot glue. For a professional finish, fill in gaps with greenery or small filler blooms. The result is a lightweight, durable accessory that won’t droop or shed. If you’re planning a larger floral display, consider pairing your purse bouquet with a **wedding flower wall backdrop** for photos—it ties the whole look together.
